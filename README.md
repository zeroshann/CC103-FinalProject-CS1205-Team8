**Problem Description**
Fast food chains often encounter significant challenges in  managing orders efficiently, particularly during peak hours when demand is high. Orders that are processed at the counter can sometimes lead to inaccurate orders and delays in handling urgent and priority orders such as express meals or special orders (Blankenship, 2025). This problem often results in customer dissatisfaction due to longer wait times. As a result, it may affect the full performance and decreased service quality of the fast food chain.

**QuickServe: Fast Food Order Line Management System**
is a C++ console based application developed to help fast food restaurants manage order efficiently.
The system allows staff to add new orders, prioritize urgent orders, process them in the correct sequence, and track completed orders.

**the programs uses:** 🫠
-Queues for regular order
-Priority queue for urgent orders, and
-Stack for tracking and undoing  completed orders if needed
-Restaurant Manager to monitor order flow, track pending and completed orders, and improve overall workflow
-The fast food restaurant staff like cashier, kitchen staff, and order handers who need to manage multiple orders
